What is a Captive?

A Captive is a privately owned insurance company established primarily to insure the risks of its owner. Insurance purchased in a Captive is often referred to as “self-insurance”. However, the industry often refers to these risk financing methods as Alternative Risk Transfer or “Art.”

Captive insurance has historically been a risk financing strategy utilized by large companies to more accurately manage their respective risk portfolios. Business risk is usually best understood by the business operators, not insurance underwriters. Small to mid-sized businesses were never afforded the ability to participate in Captives due to the high cost of operation. The advent of “segregated cell” Captives has dramatically lowered these operating costs making it feasible for smaller companies to obtain.

Recently, builders and developers have most prominently benefited from switching to Captives due to soaring premiums and diminished coverage.

A Captive may be the solution to the rising cost of insurance affecting your bottom line.
